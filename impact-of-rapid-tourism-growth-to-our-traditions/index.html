Impact of Rapid Tourism Growth to Our Traditions

Jan 20, 2023

As a Balinese, I have had the privilege of witnessing the growth of tourism in my homeland over the years. While I am proud to share the beauty and culture of Bali with visitors from all over the world, I also have concerns about the potential negative impact that tourism can have on our way of life.

One of the main issues that I am afraid of is the loss of traditional practices and customs If young Balinese are more focused on catering to tourists, rather than preserving and passing down the traditions of our ancestors. This can lead to a loss of cultural identity and heritage, and can also result in the commodification of our culture for the sake of tourism.

Another concern is the environmental impact of tourism. Bali is a small island, and the influx of tourists can put a strain on our limited resources. The overdevelopment of tourist infrastructure can also lead to the destruction of natural habitats and ecosystems, which can have a detrimental effect on both the environment and the local communities that depend on it.

Despite these concerns, I remain hopeful that tourism can coexist with our culture and way of life. By being mindful of the potential negative impacts and taking steps to mitigate them, we can ensure that tourism is sustainable and responsible.

One way to do this is by promoting responsible tourism and educating tourists about the customs and traditions of Bali. This can help to ensure that visitors respect and appreciate our culture, rather than treating it as something to be exploited.

Another important step is to ensure that the benefits of tourism are shared equitably among all members of the community. This can be achieved by promoting local businesses and supporting community-based tourism initiatives.

In conclusion, as a Balinese, I am proud to share my culture and way of life with visitors from all over the world. However, I also recognize the potential negative impacts of tourism on our culture and environment. By promoting responsible and sustainable tourism, we can ensure that tourism coexists with our culture and way of life, and that the benefits are shared equitably among all members of the community.
